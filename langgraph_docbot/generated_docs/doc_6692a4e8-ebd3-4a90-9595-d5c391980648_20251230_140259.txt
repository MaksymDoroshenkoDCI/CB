## 1. Introduction

### 1.1. App Name and Main Purpose

The project is named **E-bike** (also referred to as E-bike-de), an e-commerce platform specializing in the sale of electric bicycles and related spare parts.

The primary purpose is to create a modern, high-performance, and user-friendly online store. This platform will enable customers to easily browse a comprehensive product catalog, compare models, access detailed product information, place orders, and securely process payments online.

### 1.2. Specific Problem to Solve

The current market lacks a specialized online platform that caters specifically to the needs of e-bike enthusiasts, owners, and technicians. Existing generic e-commerce sites often have poor search functionality for specific parts, lack compatibility information, and provide a suboptimal user experience. E-bike aims to solve this by offering a dedicated, fast, and reliable platform with curated content and an intuitive interface.

### 1.3. Target Users

The system is designed for three primary user personas:

1.  **E-bike Owners:** Individuals who own an electric bicycle and are looking for replacement parts, upgrades, or accessories. They value convenience, compatibility information, and quick access to their order history.
2.  **Service Center Technicians:** Professionals who repair and maintain e-bikes. They require a reliable source for genuine parts, detailed technical specifications, and an efficient ordering process to serve their clients.
3.  **Prospective Buyers:** Individuals planning to purchase their first e-bike. They need detailed comparisons, comprehensive product descriptions, and a trustworthy platform to make an informed decision.

We anticipate a user base growing to several thousand active customers within the first year of operation.

### 1.4. Context and Background

The electric bicycle market is experiencing significant growth. With this expansion comes an increasing demand for a centralized and specialized online marketplace for both complete bikes and their intricate components. The E-bike project is initiated to capitalize on this trend by establishing a strong online presence with a superior user experience, thereby building a loyal customer base in a competitive market.

## 3. Requirements

### 3.1. Must-Have Features (Functional Requirements)

1.  **Product Catalog:** A comprehensive catalog system with support for nested categories and advanced filtering (by brand, price, compatibility, specs, etc.).
2.  **Shopping Cart:** Persistent shopping cart functionality allowing users to add, remove, and update quantities of items.
3.  **Secure Checkout Process:** A multi-step or single-page checkout process that collects shipping information, billing details, and processes payments.
4.  **Payment Gateway Integration:** Secure integration with **Stripe** for processing credit/debit card payments.
5.  **User Account Management (Personal Cabinet):**
    *   User registration and login (email/password).
    *   Profile editing (shipping/billing addresses).
    *   Order history with status tracking.
    *   Wishlist/Favorites functionality.
6.  **Admin Panel:** A secure administrative dashboard for:
    *   **Product Management:** CRUD (Create, Read, Update, Delete) operations for products, including images, descriptions, prices, and specifications.
    *   **Category Management:** CRUD for product categories.
    *   **Inventory Management:** Tracking stock levels for each product.
    *   **Order Management:** Viewing and updating order statuses (e.g., Pending, Processing, Shipped, Delivered).
    *   **User Management:** Viewing customer data.
7.  **Search Functionality:** A prominent, fast search bar with auto-suggestions.
8.  **Email Notifications:** Automated transactional emails for events like account registration, order confirmation, and shipping updates.

### 3.2. Nice-to-Have Features

*   **Product Comparison Tool:** A feature allowing users to select multiple products for a side-by-side comparison of their specifications.
*   **Customer Reviews and Ratings:** Functionality for users to leave reviews and star ratings on product pages.
*   **Blog/Content Hub:** A section for articles, guides, and news to improve SEO and engage the community.
*   **Integration with Shipping Carriers:** API integration to provide real-time shipping rates and tracking information.

### 3.3. User Requirements

*   **As a customer, I must be able to:**
    *   Browse products by category.
    *   Filter products based on specific criteria.
    *   Search for a specific product by name or SKU.
    *   View detailed product information, including images and specifications.

    *   Complete the purchase using my credit card.

    *   View my past orders and their current status.
    *   Save products to a wishlist for later.

*   **As an administrator, I must be able to:**
    *   Add, edit, and delete products and categories.

    *   View a list of all orders.

## 4. Technical Specifications

### 4.1. Proposed Architecture

A **Jamstack architecture** is recommended to meet the high-performance and SEO requirements. The core of this architecture will be a Next.js application handling both the frontend and backend logic.

*   **Frontend:** A statically-generated (SSG) or server-side rendered (SSR) frontend built with Next.js and React. This approach ensures fast load times and excellent SEO.
*   **Backend:** Serverless functions (API Routes) hosted within the Next.js application will handle dynamic operations like checkout, user authentication, and database interactions.
*   **Database:** A managed PostgreSQL instance (e.g., AWS RDS, Vercel Postgres).
*   **Deployment:** Vercel is the recommended platform for deploying Next.js applications, as it provides a seamless CI/CD pipeline, serverless function hosting, and a global CDN out of the box.

### 4.3. Integration Needs

1.  **Payment Gateway (Stripe):**
    *   **Implementation:** Use `stripe-js` on the client-side with Stripe Elements for a secure, PCI-compliant checkout form. The server-side will use the official `stripe` Node.js library to create PaymentIntents.
    *   **Example (Server-side Payment Intent Creation):**
        ```javascript
        // pages/api/create-payment-intent.js
        import Stripe from 'stripe';
        const stripe = new Stripe(process.env.STRIPE_SECRET_KEY);

        export default async function handler(req, res) {
          if (req.method === 'POST') {
            try {
              const { amount } = req.body; // amount in cents
              const paymentIntent = await stripe.paymentIntents.create({
                amount: amount,
                currency: 'eur',
                automatic_payment_methods: { enabled: true },
              });
              res.status(200).send({ clientSecret: paymentIntent.client_secret });
            } catch (err) {
              res.status(500).json({ statusCode: 500, message: err.message });
            }
          } else {
            res.setHeader('Allow', 'POST');
            res.status(405).end('Method Not Allowed');
          }
        }
        ```

2.  **Email Service (SendGrid/Mailgun):**
    *   **Implementation:** Integrate an email service provider to handle transactional emails reliably.
    *   **Example (Configuration using Nodemailer with SendGrid):**
        ```javascript
        // lib/email.js
        import nodemailer from 'nodemailer';

        const transporter = nodemailer.createTransport({
          host: 'smtp.sendgrid.net',
          port: 587,
          auth: {
            user: 'apikey',
            pass: process.env.SENDGRID_API_KEY,
          },
        });

        export const sendOrderConfirmationEmail = async (to, orderDetails) => {
          await transporter.sendMail({
            from: '"E-bike Store" <no-reply@e-bike.de>',
            to: to,
            subject: `Your E-bike Order Confirmation #${orderDetails.id}`,
            html: `<h1>Thank you for your order!</h1><p>Details...</p>`, // Use a templating engine here
          });
        };
        ```

### 4.4. Data Requirements

*   **Data Model:** Key database tables will include `users`, `products`, `categories`, `orders`, `order_items`, and `addresses`. Foreign key constraints will be used to maintain relational integrity.
*   **Data Storage:** The initial data volume is expected to be low, but the system should be designed to handle tens of thousands of products and hundreds of thousands of orders.
*   **Data Handling:** Personally Identifiable Information (PII) such as user names, addresses, and contact details must be stored securely. Sensitive data will not be stored (e.g., credit card numbers, which are handled by Stripe).

### 4.5. Scalability Expectations

The chosen architecture is inherently scalable:
*   **Stateless Backend:** Serverless API routes can scale horizontally automatically based on demand.
*   **Global CDN:** Vercel's Edge Network will serve static assets and pages from locations close to the user, reducing latency.
*   **Database Scalability:** A managed PostgreSQL service can be easily scaled up (vertical scaling) or configured with read replicas (horizontal scaling) to handle increased load.

## 5. Risks and Dependencies

### 5.1. Identified Risks

| Risk Category | Description | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Technical** | **Payment Gateway Integration Complexity:** Delays or bugs during Stripe integration could impact the launch timeline and revenue collection. | Medium | High | - Create a technical spike/PoC for the payment flow early in the project.<br>- Allocate sufficient testing time for the entire checkout process. |
| **Resource** | **Developer Availability:** A shortage of developers with experience in the specific Next.js/PostgreSQL stack could slow down development. | Medium | Medium | - Vet and hire developers with proven experience.<br>- Provide a training budget for upskilling if necessary. |
| **Organizational** | **Scope Creep:** Addition of un-planned features during development could lead to budget overruns and missed deadlines. | High | High | - Enforce a strict change management process.<br>- All new feature requests must be evaluated for impact and formally approved.<br>- Defer non-essential features to a post-launch phase (v1.1). |

### 5.2. External Dependencies

*   **Stripe API:** The platform's ability to process payments is entirely dependent on the availability and reliability of the Stripe API.
*   **Email Service Provider:** The delivery of transactional emails depends on the uptime of the chosen email service (e.g., SendGrid).
*   **Product Data Suppliers:** The accuracy and availability of product information (descriptions, images, stock levels) depend on external suppliers providing this data in a timely and structured manner.

*   All product content (text, specifications, high-resolution images) will be provided by the project stakeholder before the relevant development sprint begins.
*   The branding guidelines (logo, color palette, fonts) will be finalized before the UI development phase.
*   The chosen domain name (`e-bike.de` or alternative) will be available and secured.

### 5.4. Constraints and Limitations

*   The project must be delivered within the agreed-upon budget and timeline.
*   The technology stack choices (TailwindCSS, PostgreSQL) are fixed and not subject to change.
*   The initial launch will target the European market, with the Euro (€) as the primary currency.
