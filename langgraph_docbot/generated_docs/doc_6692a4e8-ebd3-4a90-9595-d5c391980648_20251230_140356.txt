## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 App Name and Main Purpose

**Project Name:** E-bike-de

**Main Purpose:** To develop a modern, high-performance e-commerce platform specializing in the sale of electric bicycles and related spare parts. The platform will provide a seamless online shopping experience for customers in the German market (inferred from the ".de" suffix).

### 1.2 Specific Problem to Solve

The project aims to create a dedicated and user-friendly online marketplace for e-bike enthusiasts. Currently, customers may struggle to find a centralized platform that offers a wide range of products, detailed specifications, compatibility information, and a streamlined purchasing process. E-bike-de will solve this by offering a curated catalog, powerful search and filtering capabilities, and a secure, efficient checkout system.

### 1.3 Target Users

The system is designed for three primary user personas:

1.  **E-bike Owners (End-Consumers):** Individuals who already own an e-bike and are looking for replacement parts, upgrades, or accessories. They value quick access to product information, compatibility checks, and a clear purchase history.
2.  **Service Center Technicians (B2B Customers):** Professionals who repair and maintain e-bikes. They require a reliable source for genuine spare parts, efficient bulk ordering, and quick access to technical specifications.
3.  **Prospective Buyers (New Customers):** Individuals planning to purchase their first e-bike. They need a platform that helps them compare models, understand features, and make an informed purchasing decision.

### 1.4 Context and Background

The demand for electric bicycles is growing rapidly. To capitalize on this market trend and establish a strong digital presence, the business requires a professional, fast, and modern e-commerce website. This platform will serve as the primary sales channel, enhancing brand visibility, customer reach, and operational efficiency by automating the order and payment processes.

## 2. Objectives

### 2.1 Main Goals

1.  **Launch a Minimum Viable Product (MVP):** Deploy a fully functional e-commerce platform with core features (product catalog, cart, checkout, user accounts, admin panel) within 4-6 months.
2.  **Achieve High User Engagement:** Attain a user conversion rate of at least 2.5% within the first six months post-launch.
3.  **Ensure Platform Performance and Reliability:** Maintain an average page load time of under 2 seconds and a system uptime of 99.9%.
4.  **Streamline Business Operations:** Reduce manual order processing time by 75% through an integrated admin panel for managing products, inventory, and orders.

### 2.2 Success Criteria and KPIs

*   **Conversion Rate:** Percentage of visitors who complete a purchase.
*   **Average Order Value (AOV):** The average amount spent per order.
*   **Cart Abandonment Rate:** Percentage of users who add items to the cart but do not complete the purchase.
*   **Site Uptime:** Measured in percentage (e.g., 99.9%).
*   **Page Load Speed:** Measured in seconds via tools like Google PageSpeed Insights.
*   **SEO Ranking:** Target top 10 search results for 5-10 key product-related keywords.

### 2.3 Expected Outcomes

*   A fully operational, secure, and scalable e-commerce website.
*   An established online sales channel driving revenue growth.
*   Increased brand recognition in the e-bike market.
*   A centralized system for managing inventory, sales, and customer data.

### 2.4 Timeline Estimate and Budget Considerations

*   **Estimated Timeline:** 4-6 months for the MVP.
*   **Budget:** A detailed budget will be outlined in a separate project management document, covering development, infrastructure, third-party service fees (payment gateway, email), and post-launch maintenance.

## 3. Requirements

### 3.1 Must-Have Features (Functional)

1.  **Product Catalog Management:**
    *   Admin ability to create, update, and delete products and categories.
    *   Support for multiple product images, detailed descriptions, specifications, and pricing.
    *   Advanced filtering (by brand, price, category, specifications) and sorting.
2.  **Shopping Cart:**
    *   Users can add/remove items and update quantities.
    *   Persistent cart for logged-in users.
    *   Clear summary of items and total cost.
3.  **Checkout Process:**
    *   Multi-step or single-page checkout flow.
    *   Guest checkout and registered user checkout options.
    *   Shipping and billing address management.
4.  **Online Payment Integration:**
    *   Secure payment processing via credit/debit card.
    *   Integration with a primary payment gateway (Stripe or LiqPay).
5.  **Customer Account (Personal Cabinet):**
    *   User registration and login.
    *   Profile editing (name, password, addresses).
    *   Order history with status tracking (e.g., Pending, Shipped, Delivered).
    *   "Wishlist" or "Favorites" functionality.
6.  **Admin Panel:**
    *   Dashboard with key sales metrics.
    *   Order management (view orders, update statuses).
    *   Product and category management.
    *   Inventory/stock level management.
    *   Customer management.

### 3.2 Nice-to-Have Features

*   **Product Reviews and Ratings:** Allow customers to leave feedback on products.
*   **Advanced Search:** Implement a robust search engine with autocomplete and typo tolerance (e.g., Algolia, MeiliSearch).
*   **Content Management System (CMS):** A blog or news section for articles, guides, and SEO content.
*   **Discount/Coupon Code System:** Ability for admins to create and manage promotional codes.
*   **Product Comparison Tool:** Allow users to select multiple products and compare their specifications side-by-side.

### 3.3 User Requirements (User Stories)

*   **As a customer,** I want to filter products by brand and price so that I can quickly find what I'm looking for.
*   **As a customer,** I want to view my past orders and their shipping status so that I can track my purchases.
*   **As a service technician,** I want to check parts compatibility in the product description so that I can order the correct components.
*   **As a new buyer,** I want to save products to a wishlist so I can compare them later before making a decision.
*   **As a site administrator,** I want to update product stock levels so that the website accurately reflects what is available.
*   **As a site administrator,** I want to view a dashboard of daily sales so I can monitor business performance.

### 3.4 Non-Functional Requirements

*   **Performance:** The site must load in under 2 seconds on a standard broadband connection. The backend API response time should be under 200ms for 95% of requests.
*   **Security:** All traffic must be served over HTTPS. Payment information must be handled securely according to PCI DSS standards (managed by Stripe/LiqPay). Implement protection against common web vulnerabilities (XSS, CSRF, SQL Injection).
*   **Usability:** The website must have a responsive design, providing an optimal viewing experience on desktops, tablets, and mobile devices. The user interface should be clean, modern, and intuitive.
*   **Scalability:** The architecture should be able to handle a 5x increase in traffic and a 10x increase in product catalog size over the first two years without significant performance degradation.
*   **SEO:** The platform must be built with SEO best practices, including semantic HTML, server-side rendering (SSR) for key pages, meta tag management, and clean URLs.

### 3.5 Technical Constraints

*   The frontend must be built using **Tailwind CSS** for styling.
*   The choice of backend technology is limited to **Next.js (with API routes)** or a dedicated API framework like **Laravel/FastAPI**. A final decision must be made during the architecture design phase.

## 4. Technical Specifications

### 4.1 Platform

*   **Type:** Web Application.
*   **Accessibility:** The application will be accessed via modern web browsers (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge). Full mobile responsiveness is mandatory.

### 4.2 Technology Preferences

| Component          | Technology                                                                          | Rationale                                                                     |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Frontend**       | **React.js / Next.js**                                                              | Provides a modern component-based architecture and server-side rendering for SEO. |
| **Styling**        | **Tailwind CSS**                                                                    | A utility-first CSS framework for rapid, clean, and responsive UI development.    |
| **Backend**        | **Option A: Next.js (API Routes)**<br>**Option B: Laravel (PHP) / FastAPI (Python)** | **A:** Unified codebase, ideal for Vercel deployment. <br>**B:** Mature, robust frameworks for complex APIs. |
| **Database**       | **PostgreSQL**                                                                      | A powerful, open-source object-relational database system known for reliability. |
| **Payment Gateway**| **Stripe** or **LiqPay**                                                            | Leading providers with robust APIs, developer documentation, and security.      |
| **Email Service**  | **SendGrid, Postmark, or AWS SES**                                                  | Reliable transactional email delivery for order confirmations, password resets, etc. |
| **Deployment**     | **Vercel (for Next.js), AWS, or DigitalOcean**                                      | Modern cloud platforms offering scalability, CI/CD, and managed services.      |

### 4.3 Integration Needs

1.  **Payment Gateway (Stripe Example):**
    *   **Purpose:** To process credit/debit card payments securely.
    *   **Flow:** The frontend will use Stripe.js to tokenize card information. This token is sent to the backend, which then uses the Stripe API to create a charge.
    *   **Configuration:** API keys must be stored as environment variables.

    ```bash
    # .env.example
    STRIPE_PUBLIC_KEY=pk_test_...
    STRIPE_SECRET_KEY=sk_test_...
    STRIPE_WEBHOOK_SECRET=whsec_...
    ```

2.  **Transactional Email Service (SendGrid Example):**
    *   **Purpose:** To send automated emails for events like order confirmation, shipping updates, and password resets.
    *   **Implementation:** The backend will integrate with the service's API to trigger emails. Pre-designed templates should be used for a professional look.

    ```javascript
    // Example: Sending an order confirmation email using a service
    import { sendEmail } from './email-service';

    async function handleOrderCreation(order) {
      // ... save order to database ...

      await sendEmail({
        to: order.customer.email,
        templateId: 'order-confirmation-template',
        templateData: {
          orderId: order.id,
          customerName: order.customer.name,
          totalAmount: order.total,
        },
      });
    }
    ```

### 4.4 Data Requirements

*   **Database Schema:** The PostgreSQL database will include tables for `users`, `products`, `categories`, `orders`, `order_items`, `addresses`, etc. Foreign key constraints and proper indexing are required.
*   **Data Handling:** All user-provided data must be validated on both the client and server sides. Sensitive data (passwords) must be hashed using a strong algorithm (e.g., bcrypt).
*   **Backups:** A regular, automated backup strategy for the PostgreSQL database must be implemented (e.g., daily snapshots).

### 4.5 Scalability Expectations

The application architecture should be designed for scalability.
*   **If using Next.js:** Leverage serverless functions for API routes, which scale automatically with demand on platforms like Vercel or AWS Lambda.
*   **If using Laravel/FastAPI:** Deploy the application using containers (Docker) and an orchestration service (Kubernetes) or a platform like AWS Elastic Beanstalk to allow for horizontal scaling.
*   **Database:** Start with a managed PostgreSQL instance (e.g., Amazon RDS, DigitalOcean Managed Databases) that can be easily scaled up as needed.
*   **Static Assets:** Use a Content Delivery Network (CDN) to serve images and other static files to reduce server load and improve global load times.

## 5. Risks and Dependencies

### 5.1 Identified Risks

| Risk ID | Risk Description                                          | Probability | Impact | Mitigation Strategy                                                                                               |
| ------- | --------------------------------------------------------- | ----------- | ------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **R-01**  | **Technology Choice Ambiguity:** Delay in choosing between Next.js and Laravel/FastAPI may impact the project timeline. | Medium      | Medium | Dedicate the first week of the project to a technical spike. Build a small Proof of Concept (PoC) for a critical feature with both technologies to evaluate developer experience, performance, and deployment complexity. |
| **R-02**  | **Payment Gateway Integration:** Unforeseen complexities or regional restrictions with Stripe/LiqPay. | Low         | High   | Thoroughly review the API documentation and regional availability for both providers early in the project. Develop the integration in a sandboxed environment first. |
| **R-03**  | **Scope Creep:** Addition of un-planned features during development, delaying the MVP launch. | High        | Medium | Adhere to a strict change management process. All new feature requests must be documented, evaluated for impact, and approved before being added to the development backlog for a future release. |
| **R-04**  | **Data Migration:** If an existing product list exists, migrating it to the new system could be complex and time-consuming. | Medium      | Medium | Assess the quality and format of existing data early. Develop and test migration scripts well in advance of the launch. |

### 5.2 External Dependencies

*   **Payment Gateway Providers (Stripe/LiqPay):** The system relies on their APIs and uptime for payment processing.
*   **Email Service Provider (e.g., SendGrid):** Dependency on their service for transactional email delivery.
*   **Domain and DNS Hosting Provider:** For domain name resolution and SSL certificate management.
*   **Product Data Source:** The project depends on the business stakeholders to provide accurate, high-quality product information, images, and pricing.

### 5.3 Key Assumptions

*   The project budget is approved and sufficient for the defined scope.
*   Stakeholders will be available for regular feedback and decision-making.
*   All product data (descriptions, images, specifications, prices) will be provided in a structured digital format (e.g., CSV, JSON) prior to the content-loading phase.
*   The chosen technologies will meet all performance and security requirements.

### 5.4 Constraints and Limitations

*   The initial launch (MVP) will not include advanced features like product reviews or a blog unless explicitly re-scoped.
*   The team size and composition are fixed as per the project plan. Any changes will require a re-evaluation of the timeline.
